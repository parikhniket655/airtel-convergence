In 2026, Altura Commun ications appointed a new Marketing and Sales Director

After studying customer and market data, he uncovered several important

The prepaid mobility business  had reached a mature stage with limited

sources beyond traditional prepaid mobility. 
2. Postpaid Billing Delivered Superior Economics 
Data revealed that postpaid subscribers generated significantly greater value.  
 Postpaid mobility ARPU was approximately ₹100 higher than prepaid 
ARPU. 
 Postpaid customers exhibited stronger usage patterns and more predictable 
revenues. 
 A postpaid billing relationship increased customer stickiness by approximately 
50%, resulting in higher customer lifetime value.  
3. Product Silos Across the Organization 
Despite its extensive portfolio, Altura Communications operates through multiple 
independent sales and distribution channels. Mobility, broadband, entertainment, 
banking, worked largely in silos, limiting cross -selling opportunities and cr eating 
fragmented customer experiences.  
Its Direct -to-Consumer (DTC) channel catered primarily to high -value customers 
and included a field sales force as well as company -operated retail stores that 
functioned as both sales and service touchpoints. For th e mass market, the 
company leveraged an extensive distributor -led channel. Customers could also 
purchase and manage services through digital platforms, with the online channel 
supported by the DTC organization. While each channel served a specific purpose, 
they largely operated independently across product lines.  
4. Consolidated Customers Were More Loyal 
Analysis revealed that customers subscribing to multiple Altura Communications 
services were significantly less likely to churn the services. Customers who  
combined mobility, broadband, entertainment, and banking services experienced 
approximately 50% lower churn than single-product customers. 
Strategic Dilemma 
The Marketing and Sales Director believed Altura Communications possessed a 
unique competitive advantage. 
Through its diverse portfolio of services, Altura Communications had access to rich 
customer data. Combined with advancements in AI, machine learning, and digital 
technologies, this created an opportunity to better understand customer needs and 
deliver more personalized solutions. 
As customer preferences continued to evolve, the company saw significant 
potential to increase adoption across its portfolio while creating a more seamless 
experience across channels and products. 
Looking ahead, Altura Communications faced an important strategic question: How 
could it leverage its strengths to become India's preferred integrated digital 
ecosystem for connected households?

The Challenge 
The CEO has asked a cross -functional strategy team to develop

 What are the key consumer barriers preventing customers from 
consolidating multiple services with a single provider?  
 How can Altura Communications overcom e these barriers through 
product design/bundling, positioning, and marketing strategies without 
compromising pricing? 
 How can Altura Communications use AI, ML, digital technologies and 
customer data to drive more effective customer targeting while improving 
channel productivity and digital engagement?
